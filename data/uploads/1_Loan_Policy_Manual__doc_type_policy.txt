LOAN POLICY MANUAL — RETAIL HOME LOANS
Section 4: Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio Requirements

4.1 Maximum DTI Ratio
The maximum debt-to-income ratio permitted for a standard home loan approval
is 45%. DTI is calculated as total monthly debt obligations (including the
proposed EMI) divided by gross monthly income.

4.2 Standard Approval Band
- DTI up to 40%: Standard approval, no additional review required.
- DTI 40%-45%: Approved with standard documentation; compensating factors
  (strong credit score above 750, stable employment history of 5+ years)
  are recorded but not required.

4.3 Exceptions
Loan applications with DTI exceeding 45% are not eligible for standard
approval. Any request to proceed above this threshold is classified as a
policy exception and must be escalated for senior review before any
recommendation is issued to the applicant.

4.4 Escalation Trigger
Per the underwriting assistant's configured trigger rules, any DTI
calculation returning a risk_level of HIGH (i.e., DTI > 50%) automatically
routes the case to human-in-the-loop review rather than an automated
recommendation, regardless of any other compensating factors.

4.5 Reference
This policy implements the debt-servicing guidelines issued under the
applicable RBI circular on retail lending prudential norms (see the
related regulatory circular for jurisdiction-specific thresholds).
