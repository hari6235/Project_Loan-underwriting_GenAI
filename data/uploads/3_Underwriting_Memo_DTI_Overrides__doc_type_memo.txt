INTERNAL UNDERWRITING MEMO — RESTRICTED
For: Senior Underwriters and above only
Subject: Past DTI Override Precedents (Home Loans)

This memo summarizes precedent cases where a DTI exception above the
standard 45% policy threshold was approved, for reference by senior
underwriting staff evaluating similar borderline cases.

Case A (Loan ref: LN-2091): DTI of 47.5%. Approved with a co-applicant
addition that reduced the effective DTI to within policy after
recalculation. No formal exception was required once the co-applicant's
income was verified.

Case B (Loan ref: LN-2143): DTI of 52%. Escalated to Head of Credit Risk
per the regulatory circular's exception-handling provision. Approved based
on the applicant's liquid collateral (fixed deposit lien) covering 18
months of EMI. Recorded as a formal DTI policy exception.

Case C (Loan ref: LN-2201): DTI of 49%. Declined at senior underwriter
level; compensating factors were judged insufficient (single income
source, no collateral offered). Not escalated further, since it remained
under the 50% regulatory ceiling and was within the underwriter's own
discretion to decline.

Guidance: Senior underwriters may recommend proceeding with DTI between
45%-50% based on documented compensating factors, but any recommendation
above 50% must be routed to Head of Credit Risk per the escalation
trigger, not decided at the senior underwriter level.
