REGULATORY CIRCULAR — PRUDENTIAL NORMS FOR RETAIL LENDING
Circular Ref: RBI/RC-2026/014
Subject: Debt-to-Income Thresholds for Housing Finance

1. Applicability
This circular applies to all scheduled banks and housing finance companies
extending retail home loans.

2. Prescribed DTI Ceiling
Regulated entities shall not extend home loan credit where the borrower's
debt-to-income ratio, inclusive of the proposed instalment, exceeds 50% of
verified gross monthly income, except under the exception-handling
provisions described in Section 3.

3. Exception Handling
An entity may extend credit above the 50% DTI ceiling only where:
(a) the exception is reviewed and explicitly approved by an officer at or
    above the level of Head of Credit Risk (or equivalent), and
(b) the approval, including the reviewing officer's rationale, is recorded
    and retained for audit purposes for a minimum of 7 years.

4. Reporting
Any DTI exception approved under Section 3 must be reported in the
entity's quarterly prudential returns and is subject to review during
regulatory audit.

5. Effective Date
This circular is effective for all loan applications processed on or
after the date of issue and supersedes prior guidance on DTI ceilings for
retail housing finance.
